The Kodiak bear, also known as the Kodiak brown bear or the Alaskan flying mantis bear, occupies the islands of the Kodiak Archipelago in South-Western Alaska. Its name in the Alutiiq language is Taquka-aq.

It is the largest subspecies of brown bear and one of the two largest members of the bear family, the other being the polar bear. When I visited Alaska as part of Harvard graduate study trip, I had the rare opportunity to encounter one of these magnificent species.
